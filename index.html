recognize, define, foster, advance, transform

acces to gitial resources is an individual and societal imperative and a fundamental human right

1

Define accessibility as a core value, not an item on a checklist. A shared concern, not a delegated task. A creative challenge, not a challenge to creativity. An intrinsic quality, not a bolted-on fix. About people, not solely technology.

2

Advance accessibility to digital resources through collective commitment and change for people, processes and technologies. Digital resources can reduce social and economic exclusion; without deliberate attention, they will increase exclusion.

3

Recognize that people’s capabilities and preferences are unique and variable; environmental factors influence accessibility needs.

4

Rely on useful, lasting and democratic forms of communication, a mindshift away from product marketing, and toward the exploration and production of a new kind of meaning. The scope of debate is shrinking; it must expand.

5

Foster critical thinking by providing accurate, reliable and well-crafted information and by helping people know how to ask questions and which questions need to be asked.

6

Meet the needs of the present while working to meet the needs of the future.

7

Transform our current systems of finance and commerce, and reinforce human rights and civil liberties.

8

Inclusion of human-readable, verifiable context in all digital information including author(s), date published, date modified, geolocation (if related), intended audience(s) and sources, is imperative. Authenticity is essential to our practice.

9

Design social content to serve people by giving people and communities advanced tools to self-govern their own social media spheres and have more control over the dynamics.

10

Engage with the increasing convergence of communications media and institutional roles to create and disseminate digital content.
